## Summary
The purpose of this overview is to: Describe the clinical characteristics of Bardet-Biedl syndrome; Review the genetic causes of Bardet-Biedl syndrome; Provide an evaluation strategy to identify the genetic cause of Bardet-Biedl syndrome in a proband (when possible); Review management of Bardet-Biedl syndrome; Inform genetic counseling of family members of an individual with Bardet-Biedl syndrome.

## Management
To establish the extent of disease and ongoing needs in an individual diagnosed with Bardet-Biedl syndrome (BBS), the evaluations summarized in Table 4 are recommended. No therapy exists to prevent the multisystem and sometimes progressive organ involvement of BBS. Individuals with BBS require coordinated multidisciplinary care to formulate and coordinate management and therapeutic interventions.

## Genetic Counseling
Bardet-Biedl syndrome (BBS) is typically inherited in an autosomal recessive manner. Note: (1) Although oligogenic inheritance of BBS has been suggested in some families with individuals who are identified as having clinical features of BBS and variants in two or more different BBS-associated genes , this mode of inheritance has been refuted in other studies . Identification of copy number variants and intronic variants and more precise phenotypic characterization of individuals with variants of uncertain significance are likely to provide more evidence for traditional autosomal recessive inheritance in BBS . (2) The clinical relevance of modifier gene variants in BBS is controversial at this time .
