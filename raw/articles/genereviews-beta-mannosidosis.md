## Summary
Beta-mannosidosis is characterized by developmental delay (speech is more significantly affected than motor skills), mild-to-severe intellectual disability, behavioral abnormalities, sensorineural hearing loss, recurrent infections, angiokeratomas, seizures, and ataxia. Most individuals have no dysmorphic features; some have mild coarseness. The diagnosis of beta-mannosidosis is established in a proband with suggestive findings and markedly reduced or absent beta-mannosidase activity in leukocytes, serum, or plasma.

## Clinical Characteristics
Beta-mannosidosis is characterized by developmental delay and/or intellectual disability, behavioral abnormalities, hearing loss, angiokeratomas, seizures, and ataxia. To date, 46 individuals have been identified with biallelic pathogenic variants in MANBA .
