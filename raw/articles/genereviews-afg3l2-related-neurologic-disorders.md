## Summary
AFG3L2 -related neurologic disorders comprise four phenotypes. Spinocerebellar ataxia type 28 (SCA28), the most common phenotype, is characterized by young adult onset (26.5 ± 17.2 years); the onset range is from birth to 74 years of a cerebellar syndrome manifesting initially as very slowly progressive gait and limb ataxia resulting in incoordination and balance problems. Less frequently, ptosis/ophthalmoplegia, dysarthria, or upper-limb incoordination may occur as the initial finding.
