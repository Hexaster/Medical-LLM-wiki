## Summary
Alpha-thalassemia (α-thalassemia) has two clinically significant forms: hemoglobin Bart hydrops fetalis (Hb Bart) syndrome, caused by deletion/inactivation of all four alpha globin alleles (--/--), and hemoglobin H (HbH) disease, most frequently caused by deletion/inactivation of three alpha globin alleles (--/-α). Hb Bart syndrome, the more severe form, is characterized by prenatal onset of generalized edema and pleural and pericardial effusions as a result of congestive heart failure induced by severe anemia. Extramedullary erythropoiesis, marked hepatosplenomegaly, and a massive placenta are common.

## Clinical Characteristics
The clinically significant phenotypes of alpha-thalassemia (α-thalassemia) are hemoglobin Bart hydrops fetalis (Hb Bart) syndrome and hemoglobin H (HbH) disease. The severity of the α-thalassemia syndromes depends on the extent of the alpha globin chain defect (see Genotype-Phenotype Correlations ). Hb Bart syndrome is the most severe clinical condition related to α-thalassemia.
