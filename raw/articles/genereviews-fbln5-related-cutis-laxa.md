## Summary
FBLN5 -related cutis laxa is characterized by cutis laxa, early childhood-onset pulmonary emphysema, peripheral pulmonary artery stenosis, and other evidence of a generalized connective disorder such as inguinal hernias and hollow viscus diverticula (e.g., intestine, bladder). Occasionally, supravalvar aortic stenosis is observed. Intrafamilial variability in age of onset is observed.

## Diagnosis
FBLN5 -related cutis laxa should be suspected in individuals with the following clinical features: The diagnosis of FBLN5 -related cutis laxa is established in a proband with the above Suggestive Findings and biallelic pathogenic (or likely pathogenic) variants in FBLN5 (autosomal recessive FBLN5 -related cutis laxa) or a heterozygous pathogenic (or likely pathogenic) variant in FBLN5 (autosomal dominant FBLN5 -related cutis laxa) identified by molecular genetic testing (see Table 1 ). Routine surveillance of the urinary tract for evidence of bladder diverticula and/or vesicoureteral reflux is indicated.
