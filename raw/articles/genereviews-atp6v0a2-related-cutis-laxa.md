## Summary
ATP6V0A2 -related cutis laxa is characterized by generalized cutis laxa, findings associated with generalized connective tissue disorder, developmental delays, and a variety of neurologic findings including abnormality on brain MRI. At birth, hypotonia, overfolded skin, and distinctive facial features are present and enlarged fontanelles are often observed. During childhood, the characteristic facial features and thick or coarse hair may become quite pronounced.

## Clinical Characteristics
ATP6V0A2 -related cutis laxa is characterized by generalized cutis laxa, findings associated with generalized connective tissue disorder, developmental delays, and a variety of neurologic findings including abnormality on brain MRI. This disorder spans a phenotypic spectrum that includes the historical diagnoses of Debré-type cutis laxa at the severe end and wrinkly skin syndrome at the mild end; these two phenotypes were thought to be distinct clinical entities until their molecular genetic nature was determined. Children diagnosed in the past with Debré-type cutis laxa had more severe developmental and neurologic abnormalities and a less severe cutaneous phenotype than children diagnosed with wrinkly skin syndrome, in whom the skin showed tighter wrinkles and the changes in facial features were milder .
