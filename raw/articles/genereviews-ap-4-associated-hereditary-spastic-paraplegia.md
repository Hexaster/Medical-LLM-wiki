## Summary
AP-4-associated hereditary spastic paraplegia (AP-4-HSP) is a childhood-onset and complex form of hereditary spastic paraplegia. Spastic paraparesis is a universal feature in affected individuals. Manifestations typically begin before age one year, with infants presenting with hypotonia, mild postnatal microcephaly, and delayed developmental milestones.

## Diagnosis
No consensus clinical diagnostic criteria for AP-4-associated hereditary spastic paraplegia (AP-4-HSP) have been published. AP-4-HSP should be suspected in individuals with the following clinical findings, characteristic brain imaging findings, and family history .
