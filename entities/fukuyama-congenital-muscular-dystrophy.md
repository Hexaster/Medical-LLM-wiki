## Overview

Fukuyama congenital muscular dystrophy (FCMD) is characterized by hypotonia, symmetric generalized muscle weakness, and brain malformations including, classically, cobblestone lissencephaly with cerebral and cerebellar dysplasia. There is a spectrum of severity and mild, typical, and severe phenotypes are recognized. Disease onset typically occurs in early infancy with poor suck/swallow, weak cry, and floppiness.

## Clinical characteristics

Fukuyama congenital muscular dystrophy (FCMD) is characterized by dystrophic changes in the skeletal muscle and by central nervous system migration abnormalities resulting in cerebral and cerebellar cortical dysplasia. Clinical manifestations include hypotonia, weakness, and neurodevelopmental delays. Mild, typical, and severe phenotypes are recognized.
