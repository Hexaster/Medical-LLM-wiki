## Overview

Autosomal dominant tubulointerstitial kidney disease – UMOD (ADTKD- UMOD ) is characterized by normal urinalysis and slowly progressive chronic kidney disease (CKD), usually first noted in the teen years and progressing to end-stage renal disease (ESRD) between the third and seventh decades. Hyperuricemia is often present from an early age, and gout (resulting from reduced kidney excretion of uric acid) occurs in the teenage years in about 8% of affected individuals and develops in 55% of affected individuals over time. The diagnosis of ADTKD- UMOD is established in a proband with suggestive findings and a heterozygous pathogenic variant in UMOD identified by molecular genetic testing.

## Clinical characteristics

Autosomal dominant tubulointerstitial kidney disease – UMOD (ADTKD- UMOD ) is characterized by a normal urinalysis and slowly progressive chronic kidney disease (CKD), usually first noted in the teen years and progressing to end-stage renal disease (ESRD) between the third and seventh decades . Most commonly, the presenting sign is elevated serum creatinine in an individual with a family history of kidney disease.
