## Overview

The phenotypic spectrum of GARS1 -associated axonal neuropathy ranges from GARS1 infantile-onset SMA ( GARS1- iSMA) to GARS1 adolescent- or early adult-onset hereditary motor/sensory neuropathy ( GARS1 -HMSN). The diagnosis of GARS1 -associated axonal neuropathy is established in a proband with suggestive clinical findings and a heterozygous pathogenic variant in GARS1 identified by molecular genetic testing. ## Clinical characteristics

Individuals with GARS1 -associated axonal neuropathy can present across the life span (infancy through adulthood). Although in utero presentation of a fetus with a GARS pathogenic variant has not been reported to date, it is possible (if not likely) given reports of in utero presentations of spinal muscular atrophy . GARS1 infantile-onset SMA presents with respiratory distress, poor feeding, and muscle weakness that is distal greater than proximal.

No consensus clinical diagnostic criteria for GARS1 -associated axonal neuropathy have been published.
