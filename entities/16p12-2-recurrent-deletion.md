## Overview

Individuals with 16p12.2 recurrent deletion may have a wide range of clinical manifestations. Some individuals with 16p12.2 recurrent deletion have no obvious clinical findings, consistent with reduced penetrance. Findings commonly observed in individuals with 16p12.2 recurrent deletion include developmental delay, mild-to-profound intellectual disability, growth deficiency, cardiac malformations, epilepsy, and neurobehavioral/psychiatric manifestations.

## Clinical characteristics

The clinical features reported in individuals with 16p12.2 recurrent deletion include developmental delay, intellectual disability, and neurobehavioral/psychiatric manifestations. Some individuals have epilepsy, microcephaly, growth deficiency, congenital heart defects, and hearing loss (see Table 2 ). One study included 23 unrelated probands with 16p12.2 recurrent deletion from a postnatal clinical genetic testing cohort .
