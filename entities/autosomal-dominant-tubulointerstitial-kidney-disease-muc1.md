## Overview

Autosomal dominant tubulointerstitial kidney disease – MUC1 (ADTKD- MUC1 ) is characterized by slowly progressive tubulointerstitial disease that leads to end-stage renal disease (ESRD) and the need for dialysis or kidney transplantation. The rate of loss of kidney function for individuals is variable within and between families, with a median age of onset of end-stage renal disease (ESRD) of 46 years (range: ages 20-70 years). There are no other systemic manifestations.

## Clinical characteristics

Autosomal dominant tubulointerstitial kidney disease – MUC1 (ADTKD- MUC1 ) is characterized by slowly progressive tubulointerstitial disease that leads to end-stage renal disease (ESRD) and the need for dialysis or kidney transplantation. The rate of loss of kidney function for individuals is variable within and between families, with a median age of onset of ESRD of 46 years ; however, the age range of ESRD is extremely variable, with rare individuals developing ESRD before age 20 years and some affected individuals not having ESRD past age 70 years. Onset.

## Diagnosis

Autosomal dominant tubulointerstitial kidney disease – MUC1 (ADTKD- MUC1 ) is caused by a heterozygous MUC1 pathogenic variant which results in the creation of a specific frameshift protein (MUC1fs) responsible for slowly progressive chronic kidney disease, the sole manifestation of this disorder.
