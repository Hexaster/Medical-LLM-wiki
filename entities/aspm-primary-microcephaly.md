## Overview

ASPM primary microcephaly ( ASPM -MCPH) is characterized by: (1) significant microcephaly (>3 standard deviations [SD] below the mean for age) usually present at birth and always present before age one year and (2) the absence of other congenital anomalies. While developmental milestones are usually normal in young children, older children have variable levels of intellectual disability. Neurologic examination is usually normal except for mild spasticity.

## Clinical characteristics

ASPM primary microcephaly ( ASPM -MCPH) is characterized by: (1) significant microcephaly (>3 SD below the mean for age) usually present at birth and always present before age one year and (2) the absence of another congenital anomalies. While developmental motor milestones are usually normal in young children, older children have variable levels of language delay and intellectual disability. Neurologic examination is usually normal except for mild spasticity.
