## Overview

Aymé-Gripp syndrome is classically defined as the triad of bilateral early cataracts, sensorineural hearing loss, and characteristic facial features in combination with neurodevelopmental abnormalities. The facial features are often described as "Down syndrome-like" and include brachycephaly, flat facial appearance, short nose, long philtrum, narrow mouth, and low-set and posteriorly rotated ears. Hearing loss is often congenital.

## Clinical characteristics

Individuals with Aymé-Gripp syndrome frequently have the triad of bilateral early cataracts, sensorineural hearing loss, and dysmorphic features that are often described as "Down syndrome-like" facies .
