## Overview

Brugada syndrome is characterized by cardiac conduction abnormalities (ST segment abnormalities in leads V 1 -V 3 on EKG and a high risk for ventricular arrhythmias) that can result in sudden death. Brugada syndrome presents primarily during adulthood, although age at diagnosis may range from infancy to late adulthood. The mean age of sudden death is approximately 40 years.

## Clinical characteristics

Age at diagnosis. Brugada syndrome manifests primarily during adulthood, with a mean age of sudden death of approximately 40 years. The youngest individual was diagnosed at two days of life and the oldest was diagnosed at age 85 years .
